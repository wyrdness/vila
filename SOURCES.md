# Sources & References

## Books

### Folklore Collections
- Karadžić, V. (19th century). *Serbian Folk Tales*. Various editions.
  - Primary source for South Slavic folklore including vile
- Afanasyev, A. (1855-1863). *Russian Fairy Tales*. Various editions.
- Erben, K.J. (1853). *A Bouquet of Slavic Legends*. Various editions.
  - Czech collection including vila stories

### Epic Poetry
- *Serbian Epic Poetry Collections* — Traditional guslar songs featuring vile
- Karadžić, V. *Serbian Folk Songs and Epics*.
  - Vile appear frequently in heroic epics

### Academic & Cultural Studies
- McClelland, B. (2006). *Slayers and Their Vampires: A Cultural History of Killing the Dead*. University of Michigan Press. ISBN: 978-0472069231
  - Includes vila in context of Slavic supernatural beings
- Vukanović, T.P. (1989). "The Vampire." *Journal of the Gypsy Lore Society*.
  - Balkan folklore including vila traditions
- Dixon-Kennedy, M. (1998). *Encyclopedia of Russian & Slavic Myth and Legend*. ABC-CLIO. ISBN: 978-1576070635

### Slavic Mythology
- Máchal, J. (1918). *Slavic Mythology*. In *The Mythology of All Races* Vol. 3. Marshall Jones Company.
  - Comprehensive treatment of South Slavic nature spirits
- Ginzburg, C. (1983). *The Night Battles: Witchcraft and Agrarian Cults*. Johns Hopkins University Press.
  - Comparative analysis including Slavic fairy traditions

### Balkan Studies
- Georgieva, I. (1993). *Bulgarian Mythology*. Svyat Publishers.
- Čajkanović, V. (1994). *Serbian Mythology*. Serbian Literary Cooperative.
- Kemp, P. (1935). *Healing Ritual: Studies in the Technique and Tradition of the Southern Slavs*. Faber & Faber.
